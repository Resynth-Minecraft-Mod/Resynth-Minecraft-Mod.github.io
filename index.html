Introduction

Resynth is a mod designed to provide you with a way to grow every Minecraft resource, from iron to glowstone and soon to be ender peals, while being completely configurable. The mod adds two new types of plants, crystalline (wheat), which grows ore drops that do not require smelting (such as diamond and glowstone) and metallic (melon), which grows ores (gold and iron). A plant that grows mob drops is in the works.

Resynth is also completely configurable - so everything from plant growth times to seed drop chances to bonemeal to ore generation - even disabling plant growth can be changed in the config.

Note: Resynth is still in its first stages of development (beta) - some things are bound to break, some features are not yet implemented (such as mob drops) and values (such as plant growth time and seed drop chance) still need tweaking, though you can change them yourself through the configuration settings. While Resynth is thoroughly tested and should work in most if not all cases - please do keep this in mind.

FAQ

How Do I Get Seeds?

Seeds are obtained differently depending on the plant and ore.

Seeds for diamond, coal, emerald, glowstone, quartz, redstone and Lapis Lazuli are obtained by mining the respective ore block (e.g. diamond ore and glowstone) with an iron pickaxe or better. If you're lucky, seeds will randomly drop (Configurable).

Seeds for iron and gold are obtained by blowing up the ore block. If you're feeling lucky, the respective seeds will occasionally drop (Configurable).

My Plants Are Growing Very Slowly. Why?

Resynth plants grow quite slowly compared to vanilla minecraft plants on average (Configurable growth time).

The plants growth rate is also affected by the mineral soil block it is placed on. Each mineral soil block has a mineral content - this value affects how fast the plants grow. When placed in the world, the mineral soil block has a mineral content of 1%, which can go up to 50%. Plants below 25% will grow very slowly and plants of around 1% will seamingly never grow.

The mineral content can be increase by right clicking the soil block with either Mineral Rocks (1% increase Configurable) or Dense Mineral Rocks (9% increase Configurable)

How Do I Get The Resources From The Plant?

The produce from the plant (organic ore and straw) can be placed in a furnaced to get the Minecraft resource (such as diamond).
